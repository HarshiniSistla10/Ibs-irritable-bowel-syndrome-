What is IBS?

Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS) is a common gastrointestinal disorder that affects the large intestine (colon). It is a functional disorder, meaning it involves problems with how the intestines work but doesn't cause structural damage. IBS can cause a variety of symptoms, and its exact cause is not well understood. The symptoms and severity of IBS can vary widely from person to person.

Management of IBS:
Dietary Changes:

Dietary modifications, including the low FODMAP diet, may help manage symptoms.
Medications:

Medications may be prescribed to address specific symptoms, such as antispasmodics for abdominal pain or medications to regulate bowel movements.
Stress Management:

Stress management techniques, such as relaxation exercises or counseling, can be beneficial.
Lifestyle Modifications:

Regular exercise and adequate sleep may contribute to symptom improvement.
Probiotics:

Some individuals find relief from symptoms by taking probiotics.
It's important to note that there is no cure for IBS, and management strategies vary from person to person. If you suspect you have IBS or are experiencing persistent digestive symptoms, it's crucial to consult with a healthcare professional for proper evaluation and guidance.
